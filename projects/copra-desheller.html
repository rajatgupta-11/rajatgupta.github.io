Copra Desheller — Case Study

Mechanized deshelling system designed for low-income farmers: simple, rugged, and scalable. Focused on throughput, jam-rate reduction, and operator safety/ergonomics.

Problem

  • Manual deshelling is slow, fatiguing, and injury-prone
  • Existing machines are costly and maintenance-heavy
  • Need a low-budget mechanism with field-serviceable parts
MechanismsManufacturability SafetyCost Down

Solution

  • Constrained wedge + roller cracking with compliant guides
  • Adjustable throat for different copra sizes
  • Debris ejection chute and anti-jam relief slot
  • Guarded drive; two-hand interlock option

Acceptance Criteria

  • Throughput ≥ 8 nuts/min sustained
  • Jam rate ≤ 2% over 200-nut run
  • Tool-less size changeover ≤ 90 s
  • Operator keeps both hands outside hazard zone
  • Unit cost target: community-fabricable, low BOM

Results (prototype)

  • 7–10 nuts/min depending on size mix
  • Observed jam rate ≈ 1–3% (improves with guide tuning)
  • Changeover ≈ 60–75 s with scale marks
  • No recordable injuries; guards + interlock effective

Performance varies with shell dryness and operator familiarity.

Design Notes

  • All wear surfaces are off-the-shelf; fast swap in villages
  • Drive: small induction motor + belt (easy to source/repair)
  • Frame: simple angle sections; no fancy jigs required
  • Service access: hinge-open guards; stone ingress paths minimized
